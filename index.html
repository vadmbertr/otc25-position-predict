Objective

The goal of this mini-competition is to predict the position of 5 drifters one week into the future, based on their trajectories from the past week and collocated physical fields.
The drifters will be deployed during the 2025 ESA Ocean Training Course .

Rather than predicting the exact position of the drifters, you are asked for each drifter to estimate a search area where it is likely to be found.
Several rounds of the competition will be held every week for the same set of drifters.
The participant with the most successes across rounds and drifters will be declared the winner.

Getting started

Full demo video here!

For each round (8:00 AM CET to 6:00 PM CET):

  1. Visit the Ocean Virtual Laboratory portal.
    1. Play with the displayed data and the time wheel.
    2. Draw your search areas (less than 100 km2!).
    3. Download them as a GeoJSON file.
  2. Submit your predictions before 6:00 PM CET using this form.
  3. At the end of the round, around 6:30 PM CET, check the updated leaderboard.

Detailed rules

  • Each participant can submit only one prediction file per round (participants can update their prediction during a round: if multiple predictions are submitted for the same round, the last attempt is used).
  • A round starts at 8:00 AM CET and ends at 6:00 PM CET on the same day.
  • A prediction consists of a GeoJSON file containing one "feature" per drifter.
  • Each "feature" must have a "geometry" field with a "Polygon" type.
  • Each "feature" must have a "properties" field with a "text" property whose value is the ID of the drifter.
  • Each "Polygon" must have an area smaller than or equal to 100 km2.
  • The prediction file must be uploaded using this form.
  • The prediction file must be uploaded before the 6:00 PM deadline.
